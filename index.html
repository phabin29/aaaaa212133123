Phonies · 35mm · J.D. Salinger Paul-Desmarais, Ottawa
Picture 01 of 08

Phonies

"There's a word I really hate. It's a phony. I could puke every time I hear it."

— Page 9

Holden's constantly judging people for being phony, but he's doing the same thing himself—lying and pretending all the time. His whole obsession with phoniness comes from his own insecurity and his fear of genuine connection. He wants everything to be real, but he can't handle how messy and disappointing actual people are. That's why he hates phonies so much.

02
Record for Phoebe · 35mm The Police — Synchronicity LP
Picture 02 of 08

Record
for Phoebe

"It broke into about fifty pieces. It was in a big envelope and all, but it broke anyway. I damn near cried, it made me feel so terrible..."

— Page 107

It's not really about the record itself. It's about Holden's fear that everything good and pure will get destroyed—just like he's been destroyed. He loves Phoebe so much and wants to protect her, but deep down he knows he can't stop the world from hurting her. The broken record shows how powerless he feels about that.

"I looked good in it that way. I really did."

03
Reading Instead of Socializing · 35mm Jurassic Park, Michael Crichton
Picture 03 of 08

Reading Instead
of Socializing

"What really knocks me out is a book that, when you're all done reading it, you wish the author that wrote it was a terrific friend of yours and you could call him up on the phone whenever you felt like it."

— Page 13

This shows how lonely and isolated Holden really is. He'd rather connect with a book than with a real person because books won't let him down the way people do. He can close a book whenever he wants, but you can't do that with relationships. Real relationships are too complicated and messy, so he just stays alone with his books.

04
Allie & Innocence · 35mm Figure skates, Ottawa
Picture 04 of 08

Allie &
Innocence

"I keep picturing all these little kids playing some game in this big field of rye... I have to come out from somewhere and catch them."

— Page 121

The "catcher in the rye" thing is basically Holden trying to fix what happened with Allie. He wants to be the person who catches kids before they fall off a cliff—before they grow up and get hurt the way he did. He knows it's impossible, but he can't let go of it. Allie's death is the thing that broke him the most, and everything else comes from that.

"They don't do any damn more molding at Pencey than they do at any other school."

05
Giving to the Nuns · 35mm Cash, unspecified
Picture 05 of 08

Giving to
the Nuns

"After they left, I started getting sorry that I'd only given them ten bucks for their collection."

— Page 79

This is where you realize Holden's not just some heartless jerk. He actually cares about people and wants to help. His cynicism is just a wall he built because it's easier to hate everyone than to risk getting hurt. When he sees people doing something genuinely good, his guard comes down and he wants to help too. That regret about the money—that's his real self coming through.

06
Smoking a Cigarette · 35mm Exterior, overcast
Picture 06 of 08

Smoking a
Cigarette

"I have no wind, if you want to know the truth. I'm quite a heavy smoker, for one thing—that is, I used to be."

— Page 4

Smoking is Holden's way of coping. He uses it to manage all the emotional stuff he's dealing with and to make himself look more mature and in control. But it also shows how vulnerable he actually is. He's just a teenager trying to act like an adult, and cigarettes are one of the ways he does that. It's kind of sad because you can see how much he's struggling underneath.
